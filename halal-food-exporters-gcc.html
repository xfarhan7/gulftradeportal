Why Halal sourcing matters in the GCC

Every food product entering the GCC must meet Halal requirements and, depending on the country, additional registration such as SFDA in Saudi Arabia. Sourcing from suppliers who already hold the right certification saves time, avoids rejected shipments, and builds trust with GCC buyers.

Gulf Trade Portal verifies supplier certifications so GCC importers can source with confidence, and certified exporters can showcase their credentials to qualified Gulf buyers.

Frequently asked questions

How do I find Halal food suppliers for the GCC?
Register free on Gulf Trade Portal and use the Halal filter in the supplier directory, or post an import request specifying the Halal certification you require (JAKIM, MUI, EU Halal, SFDA). Certified suppliers respond directly.
What Halal certifications are recognised in the GCC?
GCC markets recognise several Halal standards including JAKIM (Malaysia), MUI (Indonesia), EU Halal certification, ESMA (UAE), and SFDA requirements for Saudi Arabia. Gulf Trade Portal lets you filter suppliers by certification.
Can I source Halal meat and dairy?
Yes. Gulf Trade Portal has verified suppliers of Halal frozen meat, poultry, dairy, cheese, and a wide range of packaged Halal foods, all filterable by certification and origin.
Is the platform free for Halal food trade?
Yes. Gulf Trade Portal is free to join for both GCC importers and Halal food exporters, with no commission on trades. Optional paid plans offer more visibility and unlimited requests.
